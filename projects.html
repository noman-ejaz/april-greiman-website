Exploring April Greiman's Design Legacy

Design Quarterly #133

This landmark 1986 publication featured a 6-foot fold-out poster created entirely on a Macintosh computer, marking a pivotal moment in digital design history.

Greiman embraced the pixelated, low-resolution aesthetic of early computers rather than hiding it, celebrating the new medium's unique visual language.

The work blended photography, video stills, typography, and digital patterns in a way that had never been seen before in print media.

Environmental & Spatial Design

Wilshire Boulevard Temple

Large-scale environmental graphics integrating architecture with digital imagery

This project demonstrated how digital design could scale to architectural proportions while maintaining visual coherence and emotional impact.

Greiman's approach to environmental graphics considered movement through space, changing perspectives, and the integration of light and material.
