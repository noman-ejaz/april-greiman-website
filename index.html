Digital Design Pioneer

April Greiman revolutionized graphic design by embracing digital technology in the 1980s, blending Swiss Modernism with West Coast postmodernism to create a vibrant, layered visual language.

As one of the first designers to adopt the Macintosh computer, she challenged traditional print conventions and pioneered what would become known as "New Wave" design.

"I approach design as an artist. It's a way of thinking, a way of expressing your view of the world."

Her work bridges the gap between analog and digital, merging photography, video, typography, and early computer graphics into cohesive visual experiences.

Greiman's influence extends beyond her iconic posters and publications to her role as an educator advocating for technological experimentation in design practice.
